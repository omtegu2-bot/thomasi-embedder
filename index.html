Trigonometry Made Simple

What is Trigonometry?

Trigonometry is a branch of mathematics that studies the relationships between the angles and sides of triangles, especially right-angled triangles.

Basic Functions

  • Sine (sin): Opposite side ÷ Hypotenuse
  • Cosine (cos): Adjacent side ÷ Hypotenuse
  • Tangent (tan): Opposite side ÷ Adjacent side

Right Triangle Example

For a triangle with angle θ:

  • sin(θ) = length of side opposite θ ÷ hypotenuse
  • cos(θ) = length of side adjacent to θ ÷ hypotenuse
  • tan(θ) = length of side opposite θ ÷ side adjacent to θ

Fun Fact

Trigonometry isn't just for triangles, it’s used in physics, engineering, computer graphics, and even music! (From what I've heard)
